Supported networks

Three chains, one interface

Each network has its own fee profile and confirmation time. Pick the one that matches your recipient's wallet.

TRC20 — Tron

via TronLink

Lowest fees, fastest confirmations. The default choice for most USDT transfers.

ERC20 — Ethereum

via MetaMask

The most widely supported standard, with higher gas costs during network congestion.

BEP20 — BNB Smart Chain

via MetaMask

EVM-compatible with low fees and fast block times.

Process

How it works

1

Connect your wallet

Link MetaMask for EVM chains or TronLink for Tron. Your keys stay in your wallet at all times.

2

Select the network

Choose TRC20, ERC20, or BEP20 to match the recipient's address type exactly.

3

Enter address and amount

Double-check the recipient address and network before continuing.

4

Confirm and send

Approve the transaction in your wallet. The transfer is broadcast directly to the chain.

Sender and receiver must use the same network.

Sending USDT to an address on a mismatched network results in permanent loss of funds. Always verify the network before confirming.

FAQ

Common questions

Is this custodial?

No. This tool connects directly to your wallet — you retain full control of your funds at all times. Nothing passes through a third-party balance.

What's the difference between TRC20 and ERC20?

Both are USDT token standards on different blockchains. TRC20 (Tron) typically has lower fees and faster confirmation than ERC20 (Ethereum).

What are the fees?

Network gas fees apply and vary by chain and congestion. Tron (TRC20) generally has the lowest fees of the three supported networks.

Which wallets are supported?

MetaMask for EVM-compatible chains (Ethereum, BNB Smart Chain) and TronLink for Tron.
